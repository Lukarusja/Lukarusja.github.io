Time management


It doesn't matter what your goal area is, time management is a crucial part of achieving stuff in life. If yours is bad, don't worry - we have an excellent technique for you!


It is called 'pomodoro technique' and it basically requires you to think about time not as minutes or seconds but as tomatoes. Yes, tomatoes. I'm not going insane, hear me out!


One tomato is equal to 25 minutes. Keep this in mind as it is important. Now, choose a task you want to complete (write a report for an assignment for example) and before you start working, set a timer that will ring in 25 minutes. You are NOT allowed to get distracted by anything during those 25 minutes, and I mean anything - no checking emails, no replying to messages, nothing. You can do all of that when the timer rings but you will have just 5 minutes to do it. When your break ends, you have to set a timer for another 25 minutes and repeat the cycle until you finish. Seems easy, right? Just for your sake, every 3-4 tomatoes do a 15 minute break instead of 5, this will help you concentrate (like a tomato :D)



So just to recap:

  • each tomato is equal to 25 minutes
  • you can't do anything other than your task during those tomatoes
  • after each tomato have a 5 minute break
  • have a 15 minute break each 3-4 tomatoes

Role models



Everyone admires somebody including both you and me. Why are we admiring them though? Why do we want to be like them, or even themselves? That is because they have some traits we don't and we really want them.


The chances are, you admire someone because they have already achieved the thing you are after right now - here is where the next part of this stage comes in:



Think about your role models - why you admire them, what did they do to achieve what they currently have and what are they doing that you are not?
